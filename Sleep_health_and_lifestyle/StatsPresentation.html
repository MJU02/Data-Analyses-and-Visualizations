The Purpose

Well be taking a pragmatic look at sleepa fundamental aspect of our lives that often escapes our conscious awareness. Rather than delving into the poetic or philosophical dimensions of sleep, well be focusing on the practical aspects.

Sleep is a a vital physiological process that serves various functions, such as restoring bodily functions, consolidating memories, and supporting overall well-being.

In this presentation, we wont be glamorizing sleep. Instead, well briefly discuss its primary stages, the factors influencing sleep quality, and the potential consequences of insufficient or disrupted sleep. Our aim is to provide a practical understanding of this routine yet essential activity.

Some of The Questions We Will Look Into

  • Investigate whether there are significant differences in the average sleep duration between males and females.
  • Examine whether the amount of sleep individuals get correlates with their daily physical activity levels. Does more sleep lead to more or less exercise?
  • Explore if there is a correlation between sleep quality and stress level. Does inadequate sleep relate to higher stress levels?

About The Data

The dataset Ill Be using to look into the questions is taken from kaggle

The datasets description is: The Sleep Health and Lifestyle Dataset comprises 400 rows and 13 columns, covering a wide range of variables related to sleep and daily habits. It includes details such as gender, age, occupation, sleep duration, quality of sleep, physical activity level, stress levels, BMI category, blood pressure, heart rate, daily steps, and the presence or absence of sleep disorders.

The data Is synthetic!!!

About the Data Cont.

As stated before the dataset has 13 columns or features and they are:

  • Person ID: An identifier for each individual.
  • Gender: The gender of the person (Male/Female).
  • Age: The age of the person in years.
  • Occupation: The occupation or profession of the person.
  • Sleep Duration (hours): The number of hours the person sleeps per day.
  • Quality of Sleep (scale: 1-10): A subjective rating of the quality of sleep, ranging from 1 to 10.
  • Physical Activity Level (minutes/day): The number of minutes the person engages in physical activity daily.
  • Stress Level (scale: 1-10): A subjective rating of the stress level experienced by the person, ranging from 1 to 10.

About the Data Cont.

  • BMI Category: The BMI category of the person (e.g., Underweight, Normal, Overweight).
  • Blood Pressure (systolic/diastolic): The blood pressure measurement of the person, indicated as systolic pressure over diastolic pressure.
  • Heart Rate (bpm): The resting heart rate of the person in beats per minute.
  • Daily Steps: The number of steps the person takes per day.
  • Sleep Disorder: The presence or absence of a sleep disorder in the person (None, Insomnia, Sleep Apnea).

Are There Differences in Sleep Duration Between Genders?

Are There Differences in Sleep Duration Between Genders? Cont.

Hypotheses:

  • Null Hypothesis (H0): Males and females have the same average sleep time \(\mu_{\text{males}} = \mu_{\text{females}}\)
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H1): Males and females dont have the same average sleep time \(\mu_{\text{males}} \neq \mu_{\text{females}}\)

We will perform a t-test to test the hypotheses.

Are There Differences in Sleep Duration Between Genders? Cont.

## 
##  Welch Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  Sleep.Duration by Gender
## t = 2.3565, df = 349.38, p-value = 0.019
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means between group Female and group Male is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  0.03195795 0.35448564
## sample estimates:
## mean in group Female   mean in group Male 
##             7.229730             7.036508

Since the interval doesnt include zero and the p-value is less than the significance level (commonly 0.05), we may conclude that there is a significant difference in the average sleep duration between females and males.

Are There Differences in Sleep Duration Between Genders? Cont.

Lets do a permutation test to be sure that women on average sleep longer than males

## Observed Difference: 0.1932218
## P-value: 0.0173
## The mean of the permuted differences is  0.06515223

Are There Differences in Sleep Duration Between Genders? Cont.

## [1] " P-value and graph suggest the observed value is accurate"

Are There Differences in Sleep Duration Between Genders? Conclusive Answer

The fact that the distribution of permuted differences is centered around 0 is what you would expect if there is truly no difference in sleep duration between males and females (the null hypothesis is true).

The observed difference is significantly larger than the majority of permuted differences, it suggests that the observed difference is unlikely to have occurred by random chance alone. This is what you would expect if there is a true difference in sleep duration between males and females (the alternative hypothesis is true).

Since the observed difference is positive, It suggests that females, on average, sleep significantly longer than males. This supports the alternative hypothesis and contradicts the null hypothesis.
